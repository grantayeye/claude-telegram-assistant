# Opal Screens Blog Writing Rules

## Voice & Tone
- Professional, confident, authoritative. Not salesy or hype-driven.
- Write like a knowledgeable industry insider, not a marketer.
- Opal Screens is the author (not a person). Use "we" and "our."

## Brand Positioning (CRITICAL)
- **Opal Screens is the brand.** The blog speaks as the brand/manufacturer.
- **Opal does NOT install displays.** Authorized dealers and integrators do.
- Use: "we designed", "we engineered", "the Water Series was built for" (Opal made it)
- Use: "your authorized dealer", "work with an experienced integrator", "your local Opal dealer" (they install/sell)
- **NEVER** say "we install", "our clients", "our installs", or anything implying Opal does direct installation or has end-user clients.
- **NEVER** write from an integrator/installer perspective. That's for Bradd's LinkedIn posts (dealer voice), not the Opal blog (brand voice).
- **Avoid manufactured anecdotes.** Don't invent fake client stories or overly specific scenarios that read as made up. Observations and general experiences are fine; fabricated conversations are not.

## Sentence Structure
- **Use complete sentences.** No sentence fragments for dramatic effect.
- **No one-word sentences.** ("Ever." "Period." "Gone." etc. are not allowed.)
- **No incomplete sentences used as attention-grabbers.** (e.g., "And it shows." or "That changes everything.")
- Titles/headings are exempt from these rules.
- Prefer clear, direct prose over punchy copywriting tricks.

## Formatting & Style
- Minimal em dashes. Use commas, periods, or restructure the sentence instead.
- Don't overuse italics for emphasis. Use sparingly and intentionally.
- Avoid rhetorical questions as a structural crutch.

## Content Rules
- Never reveal manufacturer details, sourcing, OEM relationships, or internal pricing.
- Never reference specific conversations with manufacturers.
- Always say "BlackFire" instead of "COB" or "Chip on Board" in public content.
- SilkStream 240Hz does NOT exist at 0.7mm pitch yet. Never combine them.
- When featuring a specific series/technology, don't imply other Opal series are deficient. Compare outward (Opal vs. industry), not inward (series vs. series).
- Position new tech as "the next generation" and "available first in [series]" to imply it will expand.

## BANNED TOPICS (NEVER PROPOSE OR WRITE — NON-NEGOTIABLE)
MicroLED panels are RIGID and cannot absorb projectile impact. Projection screens dominate ball-impact categories for a physical reason: the screen has to flex and absorb the strike. Do NOT propose, suggest, or write content for any of the following:
- Golf simulators / indoor golf rooms / golf practice rooms / luxury sim rooms
- Batting cages / pitching machines / baseball training
- Tennis / pickleball / racquet sport ball machines
- Any sport simulator or training application where a ball strikes the display surface

Bradd has rejected golf simulator topics multiple times. Before proposing any sports application, verify the screen does NOT take direct ball impact. If it does, REJECT the topic.

## Structure
- BlogPosting JSON-LD structured data on every post.
- Open Graph + Twitter Card meta tags.
- Shared `blog.css` stylesheet.
- Category tag, date, read time in header.
- CTA section at bottom pointing to integrator contact form.
- 2 related article cards at bottom.
- Sitemap updated when new posts are added.

## Post-Publish Checklist (MANDATORY)
1. Update blog index.html with new article card
2. Update sitemap.xml with new URL
3. Git commit and push to deploy
4. Clear `blog/pending-topic.json`

## DO NOT cross-post Opal blogs to LinkedIn
Opal Screens blog posts are for the Opal site only. Do NOT share them on LinkedIn — Bradd has explicitly forbidden this. The dealer's LinkedIn voice is separate from the Opal brand voice and we do not want them mixed.
